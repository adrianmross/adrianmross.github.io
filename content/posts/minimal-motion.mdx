---
title: Minimal motion, useful motion
summary: Tiny interface movements work best when they confirm intent instead of asking for attention.
publishedAt: 2026-06-09
tag: Interface

---

Micro interactions earn their keep when they make the page feel alive without an in your face attitude. The best ones are cheap: no layout shift, no blocking work, no scroll drama, no surprise animation for users who prefer reduced motion.

I like motion that has a job:

- Confirm hover or focus without stealing the hierarchy.
- Reveal affordances that already exist in the markup.
- Make repeated actions feel responsive.
- Disappear entirely when the browser asks for reduced motion.
